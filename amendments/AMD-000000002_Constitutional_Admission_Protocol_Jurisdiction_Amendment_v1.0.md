## I. NECESSITY

Document 000005 requires every native-object admission action to identify a
Responsible Authority with jurisdiction over the object class and decision type.
The admitted authority structure presently governs ontology, identifiers,
predicates, vocabulary, and constitutional amendment, but it does not define
general final native-admission decision jurisdiction.

The resulting gap blocks lawful admission of Document 000010, construction of
the Hermeneutic Object Register, and native admission of Strauss's Taxonomy of
Literary Concealment.

This Amendment defines the missing jurisdiction. It does not instantiate the
office that will exercise it.

## II. PRESERVATION AND CURRENT RESOLUTION

2. Effective July 20, 2026, Document 000005 v1.0 shall be read together with

## III. INSERTION AFTER SECTION I

The following subsection is added after Section I of Document 000005:

### I.A. GENERAL NATIVE-ADMISSION DECISION JURISDICTION

1. The Repository Admission Authority is the dedicated Responsible Authority
   for final native-class admission decisions under this Protocol.
2. Once separately established and canonically recorded, the Repository
   Admission Authority shall exercise exclusive jurisdiction to issue the final
   outcomes ADMITTED, REJECTED, RETURNED FOR CORRECTION, and SUPERSEDED for
   ordinary native-object admission reviews.
3. The Repository Admission Authority determines whether the complete admission
   unit satisfies the constitutional, documentary, repository, register, ledger,
   lifecycle, and referential-integrity requirements of this Protocol.
4. The Repository Admission Authority does not replace specialized governance
   authorities. It relies upon their determinations when an admission affects
   ontology conformity, identifier allocation, predicate governance, or
   controlled vocabulary.
5. A specialized authority's determination within its jurisdiction is a required
   input where applicable, but it is not itself the final native-admission
   decision.
6. The Repository Admission Authority may return an incomplete or inconsistent
   package for correction, but it may not repair another authority's missing
   determination by assuming that authority's jurisdiction.
7. Until the Repository Admission Authority is separately established,
   assigned, and entered into the Authority Register, ordinary native-object
   admission remains constitutionally blocked.

## IV. CONFORMING INTERPRETATION OF SECTION II.3

Section II.3, Responsible Authority Legibility, shall be applied as follows:

1. For an ordinary native-object admission decision, the declared Responsible
   Authority shall be the separately established Repository Admission Authority.
2. Every required specialized-authority determination shall also be identified
   and recoverable.
3. An admission package is not authority-legible if it names only an ontology,
   identifier, predicate, vocabulary, validation, certification, cognitive
   integration, or engine authority as the final admission decision-maker.
4. Planned reference to the Repository Admission Authority is insufficient once
   an admission review reaches final decision. The authority must resolve to an
   active canonical Authority Register entry.

## V. CONFORMING INTERPRETATION OF SECTIONS VI AND VII

1. The Repository Admission Authority issues exactly one Section VI outcome for
   every completed ordinary native-object admission review.
2. The decision record shall state:
   - candidate identifier and declared class;
   - final outcome;
   - decision date;
   - Repository Admission Authority identifier;
   - required specialized-authority determinations;
   - governing specifications and amendments;
   - validation findings;
   - required corrections, limitations, or supersession effects;
   - register, ledger, lifecycle, and audit actions;
   - effective date.
3. An ADMITTED outcome becomes operationally complete only when all Section VII
   recording requirements are present in one coherent committed repository
   state.
4. Identifier allocation by CAG-000000002 does not constitute admission.
5. Register insertion without the final admission decision does not constitute
   admission.
6. Technical validation or certification does not constitute admission.

## VI. REPOSITORY ADMISSION AUTHORITY — REQUIRED JURISDICTION

When established through the next separate governed action, the Repository
Admission Authority shall have only the following responsibilities:

1. conduct final admission review under Document 000005 as amended;
2. verify the completeness and resolution of required specialized-authority
   determinations;
3. issue one authorized admission outcome;
4. require coherent object, register, ledger, lifecycle, version, and audit
   recording;
5. preserve reasons for rejection or return for correction;
6. preserve historical objects and decisions during supersession;
7. refuse admission when authority, evidence, identity, or referential integrity
   is unresolved.

The Repository Admission Authority shall be expressly prohibited from:

1. creating or redefining ontology classes;
2. creating identifier families or allocating identifiers;
3. defining predicates;
4. approving controlled vocabulary;
5. amending constitutional specifications;
6. performing substantive documentary validation for another authorized role;
7. certifying cognitive components;
8. issuing cognitive-integration decisions;
9. releasing Cognitive Memory Manifests;
10. activating production engine components;
11. silently correcting candidate objects or historical records;
12. retroactively curing earlier procedural defects.

## VII. SEPARATION OF OFFICE FROM PERSON

1. Jurisdiction belongs to the recorded office, not to an unnamed person or
   software process.
2. A person occupying more than one Repository role shall identify the exact
   capacity in which each action is taken.
3. Distinct required determinations shall remain separately recorded even when
   one person lawfully occupies multiple offices in a small Repository.
4. An LLM or Inquiry Engine execution may prepare an admission candidate or
   validation package but may not act as the Repository Admission Authority.
5. No engine output becomes admitted merely because it conforms to a schema or
   passes technical tests.

## VIII. ESTABLISHMENT REQUIREMENTS

This Amendment defines jurisdiction but does not establish the Repository
Admission Authority. Establishment requires a separate governed action that:

1. creates a Collective Agent authority record with a new CAG identifier;
2. identifies CAG-000000005 as the establishing constitutional authority;
3. identifies CAG-000000002 as identifier-allocation authority only;
4. records exactly the responsibilities and limitations stated in Section VI;
5. updates the Authority Register and Identifier Assignment Ledger as one
   coherent unit;
6. records lifecycle status, effective date, governing references, related
   authorities, and audit history;
7. validates referential integrity and separation of powers;
8. expressly withholds validation, certification, cognitive-integration,
   Manifest-release, and engine-activation jurisdiction.

## IX. DOMAIN REVIEW

The adoption review records the following findings:

1. **Ontology Governance:** No ontology class is created or changed.
2. **Identifier Governance:** No identifier family or allocation rule is changed.
3. **Predicate Governance:** No predicate is created or changed.
4. **Vocabulary Governance:** No controlled concept is approved or redefined.
5. **Constitutional Governance:** General admission-decision jurisdiction is
   defined within the authority granted by the founder ratification and Document
   000012.

## X. EFFECT ON SECTION VIII BOOTSTRAP TRANSITION

1. The constitutional bootstrap transition does not supply ordinary
   native-admission jurisdiction after bootstrap.
2. The Repository Admission Authority defined here governs future ordinary
   native-object admissions only after its separate establishment.
3. This Amendment supplies no retroactive bootstrap authority.

## XI. EFFECT ON SECTION IX.4

Section IX.4 shall be applied to require both:

1. the final admission decision by the Repository Admission Authority; and
2. every specialized-authority determination required by the candidate's class,
   identifier, predicates, vocabulary, or governing specification.

No single record may silently collapse these distinct jurisdictions.

## XII. NON-AUTHORIZATIONS

This Amendment:

1. does not establish or assign the Repository Admission Authority;
2. does not admit Document 000010;
3. does not create the Hermeneutic Object Register;
4. does not admit Strauss's Taxonomy of Literary Concealment;
5. does not allocate HOC or other downstream identifiers;
6. does not certify or integrate LC-001 through LC-022 into Cognitive Memory;
7. does not release a Cognitive Memory Manifest;
8. does not authorize production engine use or Neo4j projection;
9. does not alter prior admissions or cure prior defects;
10. does not alter the authority of CAG-000000001 through CAG-000000005.
