# Inquiry Register Entry 000001
## The Order of Discovery

Repository: Straussian Documentary Memory
Register: Straussian Inquiry Register
Status: Active Hypothesis (Provisional)
Source Context: Persecution and the Art of Writing, opening movement of the Preface and Introduction

------------------------------------------------------------

HYPOTHESIS

Strauss does not appear to teach by first presenting conclusions.

Instead, he reconstructs the conditions under which the reader can independently discover those conclusions.

His apparent order of inquiry is:

1. Establish the governing problem.

2. Expose inadequate or unexamined assumptions.

3. Broaden the documentary horizon.

4. Reconstruct the permanent conditions underlying the problem.

5. Permit the central insight to emerge only after the reader has been prepared to recognize it.

------------------------------------------------------------

DOCUMENTARY BASIS

This hypothesis arises from the order of Strauss's opening argument.

He first identifies the common problem governing the essays.

He then examines the inadequacy of the sociology of knowledge, identifies its unexamined assumptions, and traces those assumptions to a narrow documentary horizon.

He enlarges that horizon through medieval Jewish and Islamic philosophy before allowing the deeper relation between philosophy and political society to become visible.

------------------------------------------------------------

REPOSITORY SIGNIFICANCE

The order in which evidence and questions are presented may itself form part of Strauss's inquiry.

The Repository should therefore record not only:

- what Strauss concludes,
- what evidence Strauss uses,
- and what distinctions Strauss draws,

but also:

- the sequence in which he introduces them,
- the conditions he establishes before advancing a conclusion,
- and the discoveries that become possible only after earlier operations have been completed.

------------------------------------------------------------

TEST QUESTIONS

1. Does Strauss repeatedly reconstruct the conditions of discovery before stating a conclusion?

2. Does the sequence of his argument correspond to the sequence an investigator should follow?

3. Does Strauss withhold conclusions until the reader has acquired the distinctions needed to understand them?

4. Does this order recur outside Persecution and the Art of Writing?

------------------------------------------------------------

EVIDENTIARY STATUS

This entry is an active hypothesis generated from repeated observations in the opening movement.

It is not a Documentary Finding or certified Inquiry Principle.

It must remain open to correction, refinement, or rejection as the reading proceeds.

------------------------------------------------------------

PROMOTION REQUIREMENT

Promotion requires repeated documentary confirmation that Strauss's argumentative sequence is deliberately structured to reproduce the process of discovery rather than merely to present completed conclusions.
