# Documentary Finding 000003
## The Principle of Explanatory Order

Repository: Straussian Documentary Memory
Status: Documentary Finding (Provisional)
Source: Persecution and the Art of Writing, Introduction

------------------------------------------------------------

DOCUMENTARY OBSERVATION

Strauss reconstructs the nature of philosophy before explaining the historical phenomena associated with philosophy.

Definitions precede historical explanation.

Permanent conditions precede contingent events.

------------------------------------------------------------

RECOVERED INQUIRY OPERATION

When investigating a historical problem:

1. Clarify the nature of the principal subject.

2. Reconstruct the enduring relations in which that subject exists.

3. Only then interpret historical episodes arising from those relations.

------------------------------------------------------------

REPOSITORY SIGNIFICANCE

The Repository should not allow historical events alone to define the object of inquiry.

Before interpreting events, the investigator should reconstruct the nature of the principal subject and the enduring conditions in which it exists.

Historical explanation should follow conceptual and relational clarification rather than substitute for it.

------------------------------------------------------------

EVIDENTIARY STATUS

This finding is recovered from Strauss's ordering of the argument in the opening movement of the Introduction.

It remains provisional.

Further confirmation is required throughout Persecution and the Art of Writing before elevation to an Inquiry Principle.

------------------------------------------------------------

CERTIFICATION STATUS

Classification: Documentary Finding

Authority: Provisional

Promotion Requirement:

Repeated documentary confirmation that Strauss characteristically clarifies the nature of the subject and its enduring relations before interpreting historical events.
