Candidate Documentary Finding 000012

Title: The Principle of Non-Equivalence Between Prominence and
Importance

Status: Provisional Documentary Finding

Documentary Observation

Strauss explicitly warns that a thinker’s serious teaching cannot safely
be identified with the passages that are most frequent or most
conspicuous.

Documentary prominence is therefore not, by itself, reliable evidence of
documentary importance.

Recovered Inquiry Operation

1.  Distinguish between what is most visible in a text and what may be
    most significant.
2.  Resist inferring importance solely from repetition, emphasis, or
    conspicuous placement.
3.  Consider whether historical circumstances or prudential aims could
    explain disparities between prominence and significance.
4.  Reconstruct the author’s teaching only after evaluating the work as
    an integrated whole.

Evidentiary Status

Provisional Documentary Finding requiring corroboration elsewhere in the
corpus.
