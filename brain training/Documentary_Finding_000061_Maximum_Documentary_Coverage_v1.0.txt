CANDIDATE DOCUMENTARY FINDING 000061

Title: The Principle of Maximum Documentary Coverage

Repository Classification: Documentary Finding
Status: Provisional Documentary Finding
Source Work: Leo Strauss, Persecution and the Art of Writing
Source Essay: “The Literary Character of the Guide for the Perplexed”
Source Context: Strauss’s comparison of the Guide and the Mishneh Torah

DOCUMENTARY OBSERVATION

Before judging the claim that Maimonides assigned greater dignity to
the Mishneh Torah than to the Guide, Strauss deliberately assembles
every argument that has been offered, or could plausibly be offered,
in support of that position.

Strauss therefore does not test a rival interpretation against a
selective sample of evidence. He seeks the maximum reasonably
recoverable documentary coverage of the case before evaluating it.

RECOVERED INQUIRY OPERATION

1. Define the interpretive proposition under examination.

2. Search for all explicit statements, implicit indications,
   structural features, historical considerations, and plausible
   arguments bearing upon it.

3. Include evidence favorable to the competing interpretation rather
   than limiting the inquiry to evidence supporting the investigator’s
   preferred conclusion.

4. Determine whether any significant documentary argument has been
   omitted.

5. Evaluate the proposition only after the maximum reasonably
   attainable coverage of the documentary case has been assembled.

REPOSITORY SIGNIFICANCE

Documentary adequacy depends not merely upon possessing some relevant
evidence, but upon attempting to recover the full range of evidence
and plausible arguments bearing upon the question.

This principle concerns the scope of evidence collection before
judgment. It is related to, but distinct from, DF-000053, the Principle
of Documentary Exhaustiveness, which evaluates whether a completed
interpretation explains the documentary record. DF-000061 governs the
prior operation of assembling the broadest documentary case available.

EVIDENTIARY STATUS

Provisional Documentary Finding with elevated evidentiary weight.

The finding is recovered from Strauss’s sustained methodological
investigation of esoteric writing and is stated through an unusually
explicit description of his own procedure. It remains open to
qualification or refinement by further documentary evidence elsewhere
in Strauss’s corpus.
