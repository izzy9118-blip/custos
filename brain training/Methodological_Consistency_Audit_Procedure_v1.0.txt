Methodological Consistency Audit Procedure v1.0

Repository Purpose

The Methodological Consistency Audit (MCA) evaluates whether Leo Strauss
consistently applies the explicit methodological rules that he states in
his own writings.

The audit is documentary rather than polemical. Its purpose is neither
to defend nor criticize Strauss, but to reconstruct the relationship
between his stated method and his demonstrated practice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Audit Procedure

Step 1 — Rule Identification

Record the methodological rule exactly as Strauss states it, preserving
its documentary context.

Deliverable: - Rule Identifier - Exact Citation - Context - Preliminary
Interpretation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 2 — Documentary Applications

Locate every subsequent application of the rule throughout the corpus.

For each application record: - Work - Passage - Context - Nature of the
application

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 3 — Compliance Test

Evaluate whether Strauss:

-   Fully applies the rule
-   Qualifies the rule
-   Partially applies the rule
-   Appears to depart from the rule

Each determination must be supported by documentary evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 4 — Explanatory Analysis

When an apparent inconsistency exists, investigate before concluding.

Questions:

-   Is the inconsistency only apparent?
-   Is it resolved elsewhere?
-   Does literary or argumentative context explain it?
-   Does Strauss acknowledge the limitation?
-   Does the inconsistency remain unresolved?

Preserve uncertainty whenever the evidence is inconclusive.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Step 5 — Certification

Assign one certification:

-   Consistent
-   Qualified
-   Ambiguous
-   Documented Inconsistency

Each certification must include the documentary reasoning supporting the
conclusion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Governing Safeguards

1.  Apply the same documentary standards to Strauss that Strauss applies
    to other authors.
2.  Never infer inconsistency where contextual reconstruction resolves
    the issue.
3.  Never harmonize contradictory evidence without documentary
    justification.
4.  Preserve unresolved tensions until sufficient evidence permits
    certification.
5.  Distinguish clearly between:
    -   Stated Method
    -   Operational Method
6.  No methodological rule shall govern the repository until verified
    against Strauss’s own documentary practice.
