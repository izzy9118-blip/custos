# Documentary Finding 000001
## The Principle of Documentary Horizon

Repository: Straussian Documentary Memory
Status: Documentary Finding (Recovered)
Source: Persecution and the Art of Writing, Preface and Introduction (Opening)

------------------------------------------------------------

DOCUMENTARY OBSERVATION

Strauss does not begin by attacking the sociology of knowledge.

He first asks what documentary evidence made that discipline possible.

He then discovers that its documentary horizon was historically narrow, being derived primarily from modern Western intellectual experience.

Rather than immediately criticizing its conclusions, Strauss enlarges the documentary field by introducing medieval Jewish and Islamic philosophy.

Only after expanding the evidence does the inadequacy of the earlier framework become visible.

------------------------------------------------------------

RECOVERED INQUIRY OPERATION

1. Identify the documentary evidence upon which the interpretation rests.

2. Determine whether that documentary horizon is historically or intellectually restricted.

3. Expand the documentary field before evaluating the interpretation.

4. Reevaluate the interpretation only after the broader evidence has been incorporated.

------------------------------------------------------------

REPOSITORY SIGNIFICANCE

The Repository should not evaluate an interpretive framework until it has first examined the documentary limits from which that framework emerged.

Errors may originate not only from faulty reasoning, but from an incomplete documentary horizon.
