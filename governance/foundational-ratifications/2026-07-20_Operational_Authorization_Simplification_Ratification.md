I. RATIFICATION STATEMENT

I, Ismail Ibrahim, founder and repository owner of izzy9118-blip/custos,
ratify a bounded simplification of operational authorization when I merge the
pull request containing this record into the main branch.

The Repository's authorization process has become a burden that impedes the
documentary work it exists to protect. The prior framework separated admission,
validation, certification, integration, Manifest release, and activation into
serial authorities and records. That separation produced repeated permission
steps without proportionate epistemic benefit.

I therefore authorize CAG-000000005 to adopt AMD-000000003 in the same coherent
merge. That Amendment may establish a routine operational lane in which one
explicit repository-owner merge, supported by passing required CI, authorizes
all routine actions declared in the pull request.

II. AUTHORIZED CONSTITUTIONAL CHANGE

This ratification authorizes CAG-000000005 to amend the operational effect of
Documents 000005, 000008, 000009, and 000012 as follows:

1. Candidacy is optional and is not a prerequisite to validation or use.
2. Admission, validation, certification where required, integration, Manifest
   inclusion, and activation remain distinguishable states but may be approved
   together in one pull request and one owner merge.
3. Routine conformity checks are evidence, not separate grants of authority.
4. Separate CAG, DDR, VAL, certification, integration, or Manifest-release
   records are not mandatory unless a protected rule or the change itself
   requires them.
5. Specialized authorities retain jurisdiction over changes to their governed
   definitions but need not issue repetitive determinations when a routine
   change merely conforms to those definitions.
6. The Repository Admission Authority remains available for escalated review
   but is not a mandatory serial gate for an owner-authorized routine change.
7. The authenticated repository owner's merge is the final human authorization
   event for the actions expressly declared by that merge.

III. PROTECTED GOVERNANCE LANE

This ratification preserves explicit protected review for:

- foundational or constitutional governance changes;
- ontology-class, identifier-family, predicate-semantic, or controlled-
  vocabulary definition changes;
- changes to documentary evidence rules, epistemic classifications, or
  safeguards;
- destructive history changes, identifier reuse, or deletion of canonical
  evidence;
- credentials, permissions, security boundaries, or externally consequential
  production actions; and
- any change the repository owner expressly places in the protected lane.

Protected review may occur within one coherent pull request. It does not require
a chain of separately established authorities unless the repository owner
expressly requires that separation for the particular risk.
