PURPOSE

This Procedure establishes the exclusive governed process for proposing,
reviewing, adopting, recording, superseding, or withdrawing amendments to the
Repository's constitutional specifications.

It preserves the difference between constitutional change and ordinary
correction, prevents silent jurisdictional expansion, and ensures that every
change remains traceable to an authorized decision and an immutable historical
state.

I. CONSTITUTIONAL NECESSITY

1. The Repository requires a lawful means to correct or extend its
   constitutional order without silently rewriting prior specifications.
2. The founder ratification of July 19, 2026 designated CAG-000000005 and
   authorized that office to establish this procedure.
3. A constitutional amendment procedure is necessary before the Repository can
   amend the Constitutional Admission Protocol or establish the separate
   Repository Admission Authority.
4. This Procedure implements only that bounded authorization.

II. SCOPE AND HIERARCHY

1. This Procedure governs changes to foundational repository specifications,
   constitutional jurisdictions, and constitutionally defined procedures.
2. It does not govern ordinary documentary correction, native-object admission,
   validation, certification, cognitive integration, or engine activation.
3. The one-time Foundational Governance Closure Ratification remains the
   authority basis for the first adoption of this Procedure.
4. After this Procedure takes effect, all ordinary constitutional amendments
   shall conform to it.
5. An amendment cannot override a higher constitutional source unless the
   higher source expressly authorizes that form of amendment.

III. RESPONSIBLE AUTHORITY AND SEPARATION OF POWERS

1. CAG-000000005 is the Responsible Authority for constitutional amendment
   decisions.
2. Identifier allocation remains exclusively within the jurisdiction of
   CAG-000000002.
3. Ontology, predicate, and vocabulary review remains within the jurisdictions
   of CAG-000000001, CAG-000000003, and CAG-000000004 respectively when a
   proposed amendment affects those domains.
4. Required domain review does not transfer final constitutional-amendment
   jurisdiction away from CAG-000000005.
5. CAG-000000005 may not use an amendment to perform ordinary native-object
   admission, allocate an identifier, validate or certify a cognitive component,
   issue a cognitive-integration decision, or activate an engine component.
6. No authority may approve an amendment that silently enlarges its own
   jurisdiction.

IV. CONSTITUTIONAL CHANGE DISTINCTIONS

A. Amendment

An Amendment changes constitutional meaning, scope, jurisdiction, required
procedure, or binding rule. Every Amendment receives AMD identity.

B. Correction

A Correction repairs error without changing constitutional meaning. It follows
the governing correction and version rules and receives COR identity where
required. A semantic change may not be disguised as a Correction.

C. Clarification

A clarification may be recorded without Amendment identity only when it leaves
all constitutional meanings, duties, permissions, prohibitions, and
jurisdictions unchanged. A clarification that changes any of those effects is an
Amendment.

D. Operational Guidance

Operational guidance implements existing constitutional requirements and may
not amend them. Conflict is resolved in favor of the constitutional
specification.

V. CANDIDATE AMENDMENT REQUIREMENTS

Every Candidate Amendment shall state:

1. proposed title and version;
2. affected specification, section, or jurisdiction;
3. exact defect, gap, conflict, or demonstrated production need;
4. documentary and constitutional evidence;
5. exact proposed rule or replacement text;
6. constitutional authority basis;
7. affected authorities and required domain reviews;
8. compatibility with Documents 000001–000005 and later admitted governing
   specifications;
9. identity, register, ledger, relationship, vocabulary, and engine effects;
10. intended effective date;
11. prospective or explicitly authorized retroactive effect;
12. transition requirements;
13. prohibited interpretations and non-authorizations;
14. validation plan;
15. withdrawal or supersession conditions.

An incomplete Candidate Amendment shall not proceed to adoption.

VI. REVIEW SEQUENCE

1. Scope Review
   CAG-000000005 determines whether the proposal is a constitutional Amendment
   rather than a Correction, clarification, or operational change.
2. Constitutional Compatibility Review
   The proposal is compared against every affected governing specification and
   recorded jurisdiction.
3. Domain Review
   Each affected specialized authority reviews only the part within its existing
   jurisdiction.
4. Documentary and Repository Review
   Evidence, references, identifier availability, record paths, ledger effects,
   and historical-preservation requirements are verified.
5. Adversarial Review
   Review shall test jurisdictional overreach, hidden retroactivity, silent
   identity change, unresolved conflicts, and unauthorized activation effects.
6. Decision
   CAG-000000005 issues exactly one outcome under Section VII.
7. Recording
   The decision and every required repository update are committed as one
   coherent governed unit.

VII. DECISION OUTCOMES

Every amendment review concludes with exactly one outcome:

1. ADOPTED
   The amendment is constitutionally authorized, complete, internally
   compatible, and effective according to its recorded terms.
2. REJECTED
   The amendment conflicts with the constitutional order or lacks a sufficient
   necessity or authority basis.
3. RETURNED FOR CORRECTION
   The amendment is permissible in principle but contains correctable defects.
4. WITHDRAWN
   The sponsor or Responsible Authority ends review before adoption. Withdrawal
   does not erase the candidate or its audit history.
5. SUPERSEDED
   A later adopted amendment replaces the active effect of an earlier amendment
   while preserving the earlier record.

Only ADOPTED confers constitutional effect.

VIII. ADOPTION AND RECORDING REQUIREMENTS

An adopted Amendment shall be represented by one coherent repository unit
containing, as applicable:

1. an AMD record with a canonical identifier;
2. the adopted specification or exact amendment text;
3. the CAG-000000005 decision and authority basis;
4. CAG-000000002 identifier assignment records;
5. Identifier Assignment Ledger update;
6. relevant Version, Revision, or Correction Records;
7. required register updates;
8. required relationship assertions or planned-reference declarations;
9. validation results;
10. effective date, transition rule, audit history, and fixity information.

Recording an amendment file without these required constitutional elements does
not create an adopted Amendment.

IX. EFFECT, VERSIONING, AND HISTORICAL PRESERVATION

1. Amendments operate prospectively unless the adopted instrument explicitly
   states a constitutionally authorized retroactive effect.
2. Previously admitted objects remain preserved under the constitutional regime
   recorded at their admission.
3. Amendment does not erase or silently rewrite a prior specification,
   admission, decision, identifier assignment, audit record, or engine run.
4. Consolidated successor specifications may be produced for current use only
   when the governing amendment chain remains explicit and recoverable.
5. Every semantic change requires a new version or successor instrument as
   constitutionally appropriate.
6. An adopted Amendment is immutable. Later change requires a new governed
   action.

X. VALIDATION STANDARD

Before adoption, the Repository shall be able to answer:

1. Why is constitutional change necessary?
2. Which exact text or jurisdiction changes?
3. Which authority may make that change?
4. Which other authorities must review affected domains?
5. Does the amendment preserve class, identifier, predicate, and vocabulary
   boundaries except where change is expressly authorized?
6. What current objects, procedures, or engine states are affected?
7. Is any retroactive effect explicit and authorized?
8. Can the pre-amendment state and decision history be reconstructed?
9. Are all identifiers, records, registers, ledgers, and references consistent?
10. Does the amendment preserve the Repository's documentary mission and
    epistemic safeguards?

Failure of any required answer blocks adoption or returns the proposal for
correction.

XI. EXPRESS PROHIBITIONS

No Amendment may:

1. erase historical evidence or prior constitutional states;
2. reuse canonical identifiers;
3. represent interpretation as documentary fact;
4. convert technical validation into constitutional admission;
5. convert certification into cognitive integration or engine activation;
6. bypass a specialized authority's existing jurisdiction;
7. create retroactive effect by silence;
8. amend through undocumented implementation behavior;
9. authorize an LLM or engine execution to certify its own output;
10. collapse inquiry memory into cognitive memory automatically.

XII. AMENDMENT OF THIS PROCEDURE

1. This Procedure may be amended only through itself.
2. CAG-000000005 remains bound by every review, recording, limitation, and
   historical-preservation rule stated here.
3. An amendment to this Procedure may not be used to evade the founder-ratified
   separation of powers without a distinct higher-authority founding act.
