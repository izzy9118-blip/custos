PURPOSE

This draft documents the Repository's constitutional self-blocking condition and proposes the minimum explicit founding action required to restore lawful constitutional governance without pretending that an existing admitted authority can perform that action.

This draft does not allocate any canonical identifier, does not amend any admitted specification, does not create any authority, and does not itself possess ratifying force.

I. FOUNDATIONAL FINDING

1. The Constitutional Admission Protocol requires a Responsible Authority with jurisdiction over the object class and decision type.
2. The admitted authority structure currently governs ontology, identifiers, predicates, and vocabulary only.
3. No admitted authority currently possesses general native-admission jurisdiction.
4. No admitted authority currently possesses general constitutional-amendment jurisdiction.
5. The current framework therefore cannot internally authorize the act required to repair that gap.

II. CHARACTER OF THE PROPOSED ACTION

1. The proposed remedy is not an ordinary amendment under an existing authority.
2. The proposed remedy is not a Correction Record.
3. The proposed remedy is not retroactive reinterpretation of existing constitutional text.
4. The proposed remedy is a proposed one-time foundational reconstitution action.
5. The proposed remedy depends upon explicit human founder ratification outside the currently admitted authority structure.
6. Merging this draft into GitHub does not constitute ratification.

III. HISTORICAL PRESERVATION

1. All existing specifications, authorities, admissions, and audit history shall remain preserved.
2. WK-000000001 shall not be silently retroactively validated.
3. The admission-authority gap affecting WK-000000001 shall remain documented as a historical procedural defect.
4. No historical text shall be rewritten.
5. Any later correction, validation, clarification, or constitutional closure shall be additive.

IV. GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE PROPOSED AFTER RATIFICATION

A. Constitutional Governance Authority

Proposed responsibilities:
1. Govern constitutional amendment procedure.
2. Admit, amend, supersede, or withdraw foundational specifications through an explicit governed process.
3. Designate or revise constitutional jurisdictions.
4. Preserve amendment and version history.

Explicit limitations:
1. May not determine ontology classes except through the admitted amendment process.
2. May not allocate identifiers.
3. May not certify cognitive components.
4. May not activate engine components.
5. May not perform ordinary native-object admission.

B. Repository Admission Authority

Proposed responsibilities:
1. Make final native-class admission decisions under the Constitutional Admission Protocol.
2. Verify that ontology conformity, identifier allocation, evidence, responsible-authority, register, ledger, and lifecycle requirements are satisfied.
3. Record ADMITTED, REJECTED, RETURNED FOR CORRECTION, or SUPERSEDED outcomes.

Explicit limitations:
1. May not create ontology classes.
2. May not create identifier families or allocate identifiers.
3. May not define predicates or controlled vocabulary.
4. May not certify cognitive components.
5. May not authorize engine integration or activation.
6. May not amend constitutional specifications.

V. REQUIRED SEPARATION OF POWERS

The following jurisdictions shall remain distinct and separately governed:

1. Ontology conformity.
2. Identifier-family governance and identifier allocation.
3. Predicate governance.
4. Vocabulary governance.
5. Constitutional amendment.
6. Final native admission.
7. Validation.
8. Certification.
9. Cognitive integration.
10. Engine activation.

No authority may silently absorb another domain.
